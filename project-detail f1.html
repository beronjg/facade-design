Navi Mumbai International Airport F1.0

This iconic lotus-inspired terminal at Navi Mumbai International Airport, designed by Zaha Hadid Architects, features expansive unitized glass facades with patterned lattice screens for natural light control, engineered to resist extreme wind and seismic forces. Through my work with Mero Asia Pacific on the facade package, I prepared AutoCAD shop and fabrication drawings for glass panels, built detailed 3D Revit models with custom mullion families for connection visualization and rendering, designed modular double-glazed low-E units for curved sections, and coordinated with site teams for precise fabrication and installation sequencing.

The Challenge

Complex curved façade geometry was resolved using parametric Revit modeling to accurately align mullions and glass panels, reducing fabrication errors by ~15%, while wind and seismic demands were addressed through flexible connection detailing to maintain structural performance and watertightness under coastal conditions.

Problem: Ensuring accurate alignment of non-standard curved glass panels while designing a façade system capable of withstanding high coastal wind loads
